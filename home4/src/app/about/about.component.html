I am Associate Professor of Human-Computer Interaction at the Dept. of Computer Science at the University of Oxford. I teach a variety of CS topics with the Software Engineering Programme, including those on HCI, Artificial Intelligence, Data Visualisation, Ethics, and Health Data Science. Most recently, I have taught Semantic Technologies (STC), Interaction Design (IDE), and an Introduction to Ethics for Computer Science Undergraduates.

My research activities fall within Human Centred Computing and focuses on Privacy, Autonomy, and Augmented Human Cognition (AHC) through intelligent systems and smarter interfaces. This requires work at the intersection of AI, HCI, systems, cybersecurity, psychology, sociology, and the health sciences.

I collaborate with many academics within and outside Oxford; my closest collaborators are Sir Nigel Shadbolt, Sir Tim Berners-Lee, Reuben Binns, Michael Veale, Jun Zhao, Petr Slovak, and Richard Giordano. Together, we have focused on usable privacy, namely articulating achieving privacy goals in highly networked environments, achieving difficult long-term health goals (including chronic disease management and disease prevention) requiring sustained motivation, and challenges in long-term data management in an era of pervasive data collection and data silos.

I also serve as a Governing Body Fellow of Kellogg College, Oxford, and Senior Member for the Oxford Blockchain Society. Within the Department of Computer Science, I serve on the Equality and Diversity committees working to improve diversity within CS, and the Departmental Ethics Committee (DREC) to establish and promote safe and responsible research and innovation within and outside Oxford.
