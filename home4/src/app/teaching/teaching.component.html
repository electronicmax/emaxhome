INTERACTION DESIGN (IDE)

First offered: July 2018
Next offered: June 2019
IDE is a Software Engineering Programme (SEP) course for MSc students that covers a combined introduction to Interaction Design and the research field of Human-Computer Interaction. The course is based on the text Interaction Design: Beyond Human Computer Interaction, 3rd ed. and combines applied psychology (experimental usability) and design theories in an integrated course.

SEMANTIC TECHNOLOGIES (STC)

First offered: March 2018
Next offered: May 2019 (assignment only)
STC is a Software Engineering Programme (SEP) course for MSc students that provides a comprehensive overview of semantic technologies, including knowledge representations (KR) and languages, Description Logics (DL), query methods (SPARQL), and applications.

ETHICS FOR COMPUTER SCIENCE UNDERGRADUATES

First offered: Jan 2019
This mini-course, taught with Dr. Helena Webb, is designed as an introduction to ethics for second year undergraduate students in joint Computer Science courses (including Computer Science and Philosophy and Computer Science and Maths). We start with broad questions of the goals of ethics, philosophical stances on ethics, and provide many kinds of examples of difficult ethical questions that affect software developers, data scientists, AI researchers, and other computing-related specialists.
