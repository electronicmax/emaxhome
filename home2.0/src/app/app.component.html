Hello! I am Associate Professor of Human-Computer Interaction at the Dept. of Computer Science at the University of Oxford. I teach with the Software Engineering Programme, which is among the first at Oxford to offer full postgraduate degrees to part-time professionals. I teach courses in HCI, Data Science, Applied ML and Knowledge Representation, including Semantic Technologies (STC), and Interaction Design (IDE).

My research activities within the Human Centred Computing theme focuses on Augmented Human Cognition (AHC) through intelligent systems and smarter interfaces. This requires work at the intersection of AI, HCI, systems, Cybersecurity, psychology, sociology, and even the health sciences, which requires my work to be wildly interdisciplinary--which is tremendously challenging but also extremely rewarding.

Among my close collaborators are Sir Nigel Shadbolt, Sir Tim Berners-Lee, Reuben Binns, Richard Giordano, Jun Zhao, Michael Veale, and Petr Slovak. Together, we have focused on usable privacy, namely articulating achieving privacy goals in highly networked environments, achieving difficult long-term health goals (including chronic disease management and disease prevention) requiring sustained motivation, and challenges in long-term data management in an era of pervasive data collection and data silos.

Recently, Reuben, Micheal and I have been working to help end-user EU citizens to exercise new rights granted to them through the GDPR to take control of their personal data. We also are working in Fairness, Accountable and Transparent Machine Learning (FATML) to helping people understand whether decisions about them are fair and reasonable.

I have the pleasure of getting inspiration from many other HCC academics including Ursula Martin, Marina Jirotka, Helena Webb, among others.

My DPhil/PhD students include Peter West, William Seymour and Nitin Agrawal, and co-advise Ulrik Lyngs and Gatis Mikelsons; I have also advised many excellent MSc students and undergraduate project theses. Contact me if you would like to discuss potential collaborations.

I am also Governing Body Fellow of Kellogg College, Oxford, and Senior Member (faculty advisor) for the Oxford Blockchain Society. I serve as an LGBTQ+ representative on the Diversity committee of the CS department and also am a member of the Departmental Ethics Committee. Contact me if you have any questions or need advice on any of these topics.
